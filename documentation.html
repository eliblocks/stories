Overview

Browzable is an interactive web application that lets users read and upload short stories. Users can also save their preferences for authors to better find quality stories.

Registering

You must have a Facebook account to sign up for Browzable. This is to prevent spam and vote manipulation. Browzable requests the minimum amount of information, and this will be used to create your profile. Profiles can’t be modified on Browzable, the only way to change the information displayed is to change it on Facebook. Any stories you submit will automatically list your Facebook name as the author.

Browzing

Stories are listed on the front page in order of popularity. At the top and bottom of the stories area there is a row with controls. The controls on the left will filter stories by category when clicked, and unfilter when clicked again. The controls on the right are for turning pages. To view a story, click on the the title. Clicking on the author instead will take you to the authors page with a list of all their stories.

Liking

Each story has two button on the right, A button with a heart icon, and one with an X. These buttons actually affect the writer of the story not the story itself. The buttons are listed near each story simply for convenience. Clicking the heart will “favorite” the writer, and all stories from that writer will appear in the favorites page. Clicking the X will “hide” the writer and all stories from that writer will be removed from your  front page.

Submitting

You may upload your own originally created stories to Browzable. There is no review process and stories will appear immediately. Stories don’t need to be new; you can upload stories published elsewhere and you can’t take down your stories at any time. To submit a story click on the submit link on the navigation bar.

Formatting 

Browzable stories are limited to only several styling elements: Only italics and blockquotes are allowed. Browzable only accepts plain text files with markdown.

  • Italics: To italicize some words place asterisks before and after the portion to be italicized.
  • Blockquotes: Place a right angle bracket before every line in the quote.
  • Paragraphs: Pressing enter twice will create a new paragraph.
